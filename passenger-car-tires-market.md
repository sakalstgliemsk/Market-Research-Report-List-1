<p><p>The passenger car tire market is primarily divided into two types: radial tires and bias tires. Radial tires feature cords that run perpendicular to the tread, offering better fuel efficiency, improved handling, and enhanced durability. They are widely preferred for modern vehicles. In contrast, bias tires have cords arranged at an angle, providing a stiffer structure and better load-carrying capacity, but may compromise on performance and comfort. Each type caters to different driving preferences and vehicle requirements.</p></p>

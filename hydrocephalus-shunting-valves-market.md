<p><p>The hydrocephalus shunting valves market consists of fixed pressure valves and adjustable valves. Fixed pressure valves maintain a constant pressure to regulate cerebrospinal fluid flow, providing a simple, reliable solution for patients. In contrast, adjustable valves allow healthcare providers to modify the pressure settings post-implantation, offering greater flexibility in treatment tailored to individual patient needs. This adaptability can enhance patient outcomes, particularly as conditions evolve, making both types essential in managing hydrocephalus effectively.</p></p>

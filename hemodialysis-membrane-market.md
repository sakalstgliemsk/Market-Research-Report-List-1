<p><p>The hemodialysis membrane market includes two primary types: cellulose-based membranes and synthetic polymer membranes. Cellulose-based membranes are derived from natural cellulose and offer a cost-effective option, providing good biocompatibility and solute removal efficiency. In contrast, synthetic polymer membranes are made from materials like polysulfone or polyamide, offering superior performance, durability, and selectivity for various solutes. These membranes enhance the efficiency of dialysis treatments, catering to diverse patient needs and advancing overall healthcare outcomes in renal therapies.</p></p>

<p><p>Network-on-Chip (NoC) is a communication subsystem designed for integrated circuits, particularly in multi-core systems, enhancing data transfer and resource sharing. There are two primary market types for NoC: direct topology and indirect topology. Direct topology connects processing elements through dedicated links, ensuring low latency but limited scalability. In contrast, indirect topology employs routers and switches to facilitate communication among cores, offering better scalability and flexibility, making it suitable for complex architectures with higher bandwidth needs.</p></p>

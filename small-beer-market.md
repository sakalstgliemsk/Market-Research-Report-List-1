<p><p>The small beer market encompasses two primary segments: limit fermentation and dealcoholization methods. Limit fermentation involves controlling yeast activity to produce lower-alcohol content beers while retaining flavor, appealing to consumers seeking moderation. Conversely, dealcoholization methods remove alcohol from regular beers, allowing for non-alcoholic options that still deliver familiar tastes. Both approaches cater to health-conscious consumers and those desiring social experiences without the effects of traditional alcohol, contributing to the growing demand for innovative, low-alcohol beverages.</p></p>

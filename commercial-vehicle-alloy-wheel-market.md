<p><p>The commercial vehicle alloy wheel market is primarily segmented into three types: casting, forging, and others. Casting involves pouring molten metal into a mold to create intricate wheel designs, offering cost efficiency for mass production. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping heated metal under pressure, resulting in stronger and lighter wheels with enhanced performance characteristics. The "other" category may include specialized manufacturing techniques like machining or composite materials, catering to specific performance or aesthetic requirements in commercial vehicles.</p></p>

<p><p>The sound insulation windows market features various types of glass technologies, primarily laminated and vacuum glass. Laminated glass consists of layers of glass bonded with an interlayer, providing effective sound attenuation and safety. Vacuum glass, on the other hand, features two panes with a vacuum-sealed space, minimizing sound transmission while offering excellent thermal performance. Both options enhance acoustic comfort in residential and commercial spaces, catering to the growing demand for quieter environments amidst urban noise pollution.</p></p>
